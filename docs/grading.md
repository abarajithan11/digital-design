## In-class Quizzes

* We will track attendance and your attention through in-class quizzes in each session.
* There will be 2–3 easy multiple-choice quizzes per session. 
* **You MUST answer ALL quizzes** (right or wrong) in a session for us to consider you attended.
* 5% of your grade will depend on correctness of your in-class quiz answers.

## Discord Participation

* We will have a Discord server for this class, and we will create channels per lectures, discussions, assignment, exam preparation, etc.
* 5% of your grade depends on how much you engage with your peers and us on Discord.

## Attendance

* In-person attendance (answering ALL quizzes) is mandatory for every session.
* Since this course is fast-paced, missing even a single session (= lecture/discussion) will make you quickly lose track.
* 5% will be subtracted from your grades **per session** missed.
* You may miss **AT MOST TWO** in-person sessions and recover the 5% penalty points by submitting a printed set of slides of the missed session, with **sufficient** handwritten annotations on each slide on what you learned from watching the recording, within four days of missing the session.

## Assignments

Programming assignments will be auto-graded. We will run them through two testbenches: one that we give you and one that we keep secret. Your grades will depend on the number of test cases you pass and the elegance of your code.

## Final Exam

Final exam will be in-person, paper-based. Most questions will include SystemVerilog code related to your assignments. If you typed and debugged the assignments by yourself, you can easily get 100% in the final exam. Therefore, do not use AI to complete your assignments.

## AI use

* Since this is a beginner-level course, any use of AI will impede your learning. 
* Once you learn the fundamentals (after this course), AI can be an excellent tool to boost your productivity.
* Avoid using IDE-based AIs for this course. You may ask targeted questions using chat-based online AI tools, if you really want to.
* If you do your assignments (design, write code, debug, etc.) using AI, you may not be able to pass the paper-based exam worth 30% of your grade.
